Before install matters.

Visible manufacturing defects must be reported before the flooring is installed.

What is covered

Wear-through and structure, when installed correctly.

01
Manufacturing defects

Coverage includes qualifying warping, cracking, delaminating, and defects from improper milling, grading, staining, or coating.

02
Wear-through

The plank wear layer is warranted not to wear through under normal use for the applicable warranty period.

03
Topical moisture

Surface spills are covered when water does not travel over room edges, cut boards, or other openings into the subfloor.

04
Replacement material

Approved claims are resolved with new materials of similar grade for repair or replacement of affected areas.

Important limits

What the warranty does not cover.

These limits are the part most customers need before they buy, install, or file a claim.

Installation or labor costs

Removal, reinstallation, labor, phone calls, materials, inconvenience, and incidental or consequential expenses are not covered.

Scratches, dents, gloss loss

Gloss reduction, surface scratches, gouges, scuffs, punctures, cuts, indentations, burns, and ordinary wear are excluded.

Subfloor moisture

Damage from moisture under the flooring, mildew, alkaline substances, hydrostatic pressure, or humidity outside guidelines is not covered.

Improper use or care

Noncompliant installation, abuse, pets, cleats, roller skates, and improper maintenance are excluded. See the installation requirements below.

Natural color variation

Variation from samples, replacement material, illustrations, sunlight exposure, UV light, age, pattern, texture, or natural components is expected.

Wrong location or product

Flooring installed in inappropriate locations, outside dry climate-controlled indoor areas, or using wall and ceiling planks as flooring is excluded.

Whoever installs it

Nine requirements. The warranty rides on them.

To maintain your warranty, follow these manufacturer requirements.

Transition moldings

Use transition moldings at exterior doors and thresholds.

1/4″ expansion gap

Maintain a minimum 1/4″ expansion gap around all walls and fixed objects.

Flat to 3/16″ per 8 ft

Ensure the subfloor is flat within 3/16″ over an 8-foot span.

48-hour acclimation

Acclimate flooring onsite for at least 48 hours in a climate-controlled environment.

Cabinets first

Do not install cabinets or other permanent fixtures on top of the floating floor.

6 mil moisture barrier

Over concrete subfloors, install a 6 mil moisture barrier.

No hardwood underneath

Do not install over existing hardwood flooring.

Glue only where required

Do not glue planks except where specifically required for stair treads or transition pieces.

No added underlayment

Do not install additional underlayment unless specifically approved by the manufacturer, as doing so may void the warranty and damage the locking system.

Failure to follow these installation requirements may result in product failure and may void the manufacturer's warranty.
