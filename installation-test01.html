01

Confirm the product before work begins.

Check style, color, quantity, and carton condition the day the order arrives — not install day. Save carton labels, order records, and photos with your warranty documentation.

  • Blend planks from several cartons for a natural, even layout
  • Let cartons acclimate indoors, in the room's normal climate
  • Report visible defects before installation — installed planks are accepted planks
02

The subfloor decides how good the floor feels.

Hard, sound, clean, smooth, flat, and dry — all six, no exceptions. Correct dips, high spots, adhesive residue, debris, and movement first. An uneven subfloor makes a floating floor sound hollow, flex, or unlock.

Skip the extra padding. Our planks ship with an attached 1.5 mm pad. Adding more underlayment voids the warranty unless the product's install guide explicitly allows it.
03

Moisture problems come from below.

A rigid waterproof core shrugs off spills from above — it can't stop vapor from underneath. Test and document concrete and wood substrates before installing. New concrete must be fully cured.

  • Moisture test every substrate, and keep the readings with your records
  • Fix hydrostatic and drainage problems before flooring, not after
  • Indoors only — dry, climate-controlled spaces
04

Plan the layout before you lock a joint.

Dry-lay a few rows first. Start from a straight wall, stagger end joints so the pattern reads naturally, and check that the last row won't end in a sliver.

  • Expansion gaps at every wall, column, jamb, and transition
  • Undercut door jambs so planks slide underneath cleanly
  • Cabinets go in first — never pin a floating floor underneath them
05

Lock the floor together, row by row.

Use spacers against the walls, engage each plank fully before moving on, and tap joints closed with a tapping block — never a bare hammer on the plank edge. A pull bar closes the last plank in each row.

Stairs are a different job. They need stair nose, trim, and an adhesive method per the product guidance — not a standard floating install. Ask us before ordering for stairs.
06

Finish the edges, keep the leftovers.

Reinstall baseboards or trim without fastening through the flooring — the floor needs to move freely under it. Then keep the extra cartons: attic stock from the original production run is the best match you'll ever get for a future repair.

  • Trim attaches to the wall, never through the plank
  • Felt pads under furniture, walk-off mats at entries
  • Store attic stock flat, indoors, away from moisture
