01

Confirm the product before work begins.

Check style, color, quantity, and carton condition the day the order arrives — not install day. Save carton labels, order records, and photos with your warranty documentation.

  • Acclimate flooring onsite for at least 48 hours, in a climate-controlled environment
  • Blend planks from several cartons for a natural, even layout
  • Report visible defects before installation — installed planks are accepted planks
02

The subfloor decides how good the floor feels.

Hard, sound, clean, smooth, flat, and dry — all six, no exceptions. Flat means within 3/16″ over an 8-foot span: correct dips, high spots, adhesive residue, debris, and movement first. An uneven subfloor makes a floating floor sound hollow, flex, or unlock.

  • Flat within 3/16″ over an 8-foot span — verify before the first row
  • Never install over existing hardwood flooring
Skip the extra padding. Our planks ship with an attached 1.5 mm pad. Additional underlayment isn't approved by the manufacturer — it can damage the locking system and may void the warranty.
03

Moisture problems come from below.

A rigid waterproof core shrugs off spills from above — it can't stop vapor from underneath. Test and document concrete and wood substrates before installing. New concrete must be fully cured.

  • Over concrete subfloors, install a 6 mil moisture barrier
  • Moisture test every substrate, and keep the readings with your records
  • Fix hydrostatic and drainage problems before flooring, not after
  • Indoors only — dry, climate-controlled spaces
04

Plan the layout before you lock a joint.

Dry-lay a few rows first. Start from a straight wall, stagger end joints so the pattern reads naturally, and check that the last row won't end in a sliver.

  • Keep a minimum 1/4″ expansion gap at every wall and fixed object
  • Use transition moldings at exterior doors and thresholds
  • Undercut door jambs so planks slide underneath cleanly
  • Cabinets and permanent fixtures go in first — never on top of a floating floor
05

Lock the floor together, row by row.

Use spacers against the walls, engage each plank fully before moving on, and tap joints closed with a tapping block — never a bare hammer on the plank edge. A pull bar closes the last plank in each row.

Glue is the exception, not the rule. Planks are never glued down — except where specifically required for stair treads and transition pieces. Stairs also need stair nose and trim per the product guidance. Ask us before ordering for stairs.
06

Finish the edges, keep the leftovers.

Reinstall baseboards or trim without fastening through the flooring — the floor needs to move freely under it. Then keep the extra cartons: attic stock from the original production run is the best match you'll ever get for a future repair.

  • Trim attaches to the wall, never through the plank
  • Felt pads under furniture, walk-off mats at entries
  • Store attic stock flat, indoors, away from moisture

DIY or pro

Confident click-lock installs are DIY-friendly.

Many of our floating floors go down beautifully for a careful DIYer with the right prep and patience. For stairs, glue-down, large connected spaces, or a tricky subfloor, a professional installer is worth it.

Either way, order a little extra for cuts, closets, and future repairs — and keep the leftover cartons as attic stock.

Whoever installs it

Nine requirements. The warranty rides on them.

To maintain your warranty, follow these manufacturer requirements.

01

Transition moldings

Use transition moldings at exterior doors and thresholds.

02

1/4″ expansion gap

Maintain a minimum 1/4″ expansion gap around all walls and fixed objects.

03

Flat to 3/16″ per 8 ft

Ensure the subfloor is flat within 3/16″ over an 8-foot span.

04

48-hour acclimation

Acclimate flooring onsite for at least 48 hours in a climate-controlled environment.

05

Cabinets first

Do not install cabinets or other permanent fixtures on top of the floating floor.

06

6 mil moisture barrier

Over concrete subfloors, install a 6 mil moisture barrier.

07

No hardwood underneath

Do not install over existing hardwood flooring.

08

Glue only where required

Do not glue planks except where specifically required for stair treads or transition pieces.

09

No added underlayment

Do not install additional underlayment unless specifically approved by the manufacturer — doing so may void the warranty and damage the locking system.

Failure to follow these installation requirements may result in product failure and may void the manufacturer's warranty.
