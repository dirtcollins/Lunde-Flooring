Installation guide

Plan the room before the first plank clicks.

A good floor starts with a clean, flat, dry subfloor, stable job-site conditions, and a layout that respects expansion space around fixed surfaces.

Installer checklist Inspect, acclimate, moisture test — then install.

Never install visible defects. Save carton labels, site notes, and photos with the order record for your warranty.

Before you begin

Four things to get right first.

01

Confirm the product

Check style, color, quantity, and carton condition before work begins. Blend cartons for a natural, even layout.

02

Prepare the subfloor

Subfloors must be hard, sound, clean, smooth, flat, and dry. Correct dips, high spots, debris, and movement.

03

Control moisture

Test and document concrete and wood substrates. New concrete must be fully cured before flooring goes down.

04

Leave expansion room

Keep expansion gaps at walls, columns, jambs, and transitions. Never pin a floating floor under cabinets.

Installing rows

Dry-lay first, then lock the floor together row by row.

Start from a straight wall, use spacers, and stagger end joints for a natural look. Make sure each plank is fully engaged before continuing.

  • Undercut door jambs so planks slide underneath
  • Install trim without fastening through the floor
  • Keep the required expansion gap around the whole perimeter

DIY or pro

Confident click-lock installs are DIY-friendly.

Many of our floating floors go down beautifully for a careful DIYer with the right prep and patience. For stairs, glue-down, large connected spaces, or a tricky subfloor, a professional installer is worth it.

Either way, order a little extra for cuts, closets, and future repairs — and keep the leftover cartons as attic stock.
