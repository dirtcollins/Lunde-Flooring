The job at a glance

Six steps, in order. Skip none.

STEP 01

Confirm & acclimate

Check style, color, quantity, and carton condition. Let cartons acclimate in the room's normal climate.

STEP 02

Prep the subfloor

Hard, sound, clean, smooth, flat, dry. Fix dips, high spots, residue, and movement first.

STEP 03

Moisture test

Test and document concrete and wood substrates. New concrete must be fully cured.

STEP 04

Plan the layout

Dry-lay from a straight wall, stagger joints, blend cartons, avoid sliver rows.

STEP 05

Lock the rows

Spacers at walls, jambs undercut, tapping block and pull bar. Engage every joint fully.

STEP 06

Finish & protect

Trim to the wall, never through the floor. Keep attic stock. Felt pads and entry mats.

"A floating floor never forgives a bad subfloor. Flat and dry first — everything else is easy." The one rule our install team repeats

Before you ask

The install questions we hear most.

Can I install over my existing floor?

Some hard surfaces are fine if they're secure, flat, clean, and dry. Never over carpet or cushioned flooring, and never over anything that moves or traps moisture.

Do I need underlayment?

No — and don't add any. The 1.5 mm IXPE pad is already attached on applicable products, and extra underlayment voids the warranty unless the product's install guide allows it.

Can it go on stairs?

Only with the correct stair nose, trim, and adhesive method. Stairs aren't a standard floating install — talk to us before ordering for them.

Can cabinets sit on the new floor?

No. Floating floors need to move. Cabinets go in first; the floor is installed around them with expansion space.

How much extra should I order?

Enough for cuts, closets, layout waste, and a couple of spare cartons of attic stock — future repairs match best from the original production run.
