Installing it yourself, or hiring it out?

The DIY route

Click-lock floors reward patience, not muscle.

Acclimate and inspect

Bring cartons into the room's normal climate, then check style, color, quantity, and condition. Never install a visibly defective plank — report it first.

Prep the subfloor

Hard, sound, clean, smooth, flat, dry. Fix dips and high spots, clear debris and residue. No extra underlayment — the 1.5 mm pad is already attached.

Moisture test

Test concrete and wood substrates and keep the readings. New concrete must be fully cured. Waterproof planks don't fix a wet slab.

Dry-lay the layout

Start from a straight wall, stagger the end joints, blend planks from multiple cartons, and make sure the last row isn't a sliver.

Lock rows with spacers in

Expansion gaps at every wall, jamb, column, and transition. Undercut door jambs. Tapping block and pull bar — never a bare hammer on the joint.

Trim without pinning

Baseboards fasten to the wall, not through the floor. Keep leftover cartons as attic stock for future repairs.

Good DIY projects

  • Open rectangular rooms over a prepared subfloor
  • Floating click-lock installs at ground level or above
  • Rooms where cabinets are already in place to floor around

Call a pro instead

  • Stairs — they need stair nose and an adhesive method
  • Glue-down installs and large connected spaces
  • Out-of-flat subfloors or moisture problems below

Working with an installer

Your job is the five questions, not the tapping block.

Ask about subfloor prep

What will they do to get it hard, clean, flat, and dry — and is that work in the quote? Prep is where good installs are won.

Ask for the moisture readings

Concrete and wood substrates should be tested and documented before day one. Keep those readings with your order records.

Confirm expansion space

Gaps at every wall, jamb, and transition, jambs undercut, and no fasteners through a floating floor — including under trim.

Confirm no added underlayment

The pad is attached to the plank. Extra underlayment under our floors voids the warranty unless the product guide allows it.

Schedule delivery before labor

Freight arrives curbside. Get cartons indoors and acclimating before the installer is on the clock — and inspect everything before install day.
